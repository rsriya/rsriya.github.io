What else do we have in the Solar System besides the Sun and planets?
We have comets, meteorites, and asteroids. Comets come from Kupler belt. Kuiper Belt (ky-purr) is a flat ring of millions of small, icy objects just outside of Neptune’s orbit. This is where you’ll find dwarf planet Pluto. These objects orbit the Sun at a very great distance. They are mostly 30 to 50 times farther from the Sun than Earth is. They take less than 200 years to orbit the Sun. At the outer reaches of the solar system is the Oort Cloud. It is a massive cloud of countless small, icy objects. The Oort Cloud surrounds the rest of the solar system. It is about 50 times farther from the Sun than the Kuiper Belt. The comet with the most extended known orbit takes over 250,000 years to make just one trip around the Sun!
